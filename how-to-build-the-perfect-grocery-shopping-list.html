How to build the perfect grocery shopping list

Released on 26th May 2014

Grocery shopping lists are the basis of a well-budgeted grocery shopping experience. But how does one build the perfect grocery-shopping list? Our research team suggests some tips on how you can make the most of your online grocery shopping experience at eshop.tesco.com.my.

Put it on paper, not memory
Unplanned spending is a common problem among many shoppers: A survey in the UK found that shoppers overspend by GBP30 in impulse and unplanned purchases. That money can be saved by simply preparing a grocery shopping list on paper, listing down the most affordable items of the week, and sticking to it. Don’t just commit the list to memory: having it down on paper keeps you focused by reducing the stress of having to constantly recall the 30-something items.

Plan to shop by the week
How do you decide what groceries are important for the week? One way is to plan your meals for the week before going to the aisles. Sticking to a pre-planned diet for a week may prove to be difficult for most, but using tools like Evernote Food can make meal planning and shopping effective and fun.

Check out what you have in the fridge
How many of us have uncovered items we bought a year ago, only to leave it neglected in the fridge for months? Some studies note between 14-25% of the food we bring home from the grocery store ends up in the trash. So before you develop your grocery list and shop online, see where you can reuse or get more meals out of what you already have.

Plan to eat around groceries that are on sale
Each week brings different prices and offers for market goods – so you could save a fair amount by planning your meals around items that are on sale that week. Among the mobile apps that can help you achieve proper meal planning include Ziplist and Paprika Recipe Manager, which combine both grocery shopping and meal planning in one convenient app.
