What kind of grocery shopper are you?

Released on 14th June 2014

In its effort to maximize the customer’s grocery shopping experience, the Tesco Delivery Labs team identified 4 types of Malaysian shoppers, and suggested how each group can use online grocery shopping to improve their shopping habits.

Can you spot which kind of shopper you are?

The Bargain Hunter
The bargain hunter is often seen diligently searching newspapers, magazines, online for any coupons, deals, or offers. However, their blind pursuit for the best prices will often lead them to buying things they have no use for.

Pros: Seldom does a deal or promotion go unnoticed under their watchful eyes.

Cons: Bargain hunters may start hoarding items that aren’t needed. Excuses such as "Just in case" "It's on sale" and "You'll never know" are often used to justify their purchases.

How to optimise:  Bargain hunters should create a shopping list to ensure that only the things that they need are purchased. Additionally, real-time price counters on online shopping sites will help keep shoppers mindful of the budget being spent.

The Impulsive Buyer
The impulsive shopper is driven by primal instincts to buy shiny and colourful things – regardless of necessity or price. In extreme cases, impulsive shopping may lead to increased debts and emotional distress.

Pros: Impulsive buyers are easy-going shoppers who are willing to try new products, brands and recipes. There's never a dull moment shopping with one.

Cons: Extreme impulsive shoppers are likely to make irrational purchases based on attractiveness, with secondary consideration for price, value, and utility. Racking up a high credit card bill is often a danger.

How to optimise: With over 17,000 items available at Tesco’s eshop, shopping online provides a satisfactory level of choice, while running a lower risk of being distracted by new and shiny items.

The Analytical Worrier
These fickle-minded shoppers often feel overwhelmed by considering too many factors – including price, quality, quantity, and nutritional value – before placing an item in the basket.

Pros: They will only purchase items that they are absolutely certain they need.

Cons: Shopping with one is never a happy experience – an analytical shopper will drive most people up the wall with their infuriating indecisiveness.

How to optimise: Skip the dithering on the aisles, and carry out the necessary research and purchasing online. Shopping this way will satisfy the natural instincts of the analytical shopper without bothering anyone else.

The Missionary (Wo)Man
These shoppers are the most efficient of all groups. They often only visit stores with a prepared list of items, and walk out with the exact things that they were there for.

Pros: These shoppers will get the items within the time frame that they have allocated for. They are focused and efficient, and spend the least amount of time in the store

Cons: They tend to miss out on the weekly deals and promotions that have been advertised in stores and/or online.

How to optimise: Shopping for goods online may prove beneficial to this category of shopper, as they are able to purchase their intended goods, and still browse through deals.
