4 surprising ways online shopping can benefit your love life

Released on 26th May 2014

Among our recent discoveries include a possible link that online shopping may prove beneficial to a long-lasting and healthy relationship. How? The team came up with several theories.

Online shopping saves your sanity
Market research by Frost and Sullivan showed that 41% of KL-based respondents ranked traffic jam and congestion as their number one frustrations – a likely cause for a heightened amount of stress, a major relationship killer. Evidence suggests that utilizing home delivery services may reduce heated arguments that begin with “Are we there yet? Why aren’t we there yet?”

Make time for more getaways
According to a PayPal survey, it is expected that a Malaysian will save an average of 3 hours each time they shop online instead of going to a shopping mall. That amount of time could be spent in activities that can improve a relationship: taking a drive out of the city, having a romantic night in, or cleaning the house.

Learn to budget better
As much as couples hate to admit it, monetary issues are often at the heart of many breakups. By offering precise price calculations before checking out, online shopping enables couples to practice better budgeting habits, be more responsible, and provide evidence of who bought extra lipstick that week.

Make surprise deliveries
If your partner has been having a bad day, make it better by organizing a surprise delivery of his/her favourite things. Use the Tesco Delivery Truck to deliver a comforting tub of ice cream or chocolates, or a basketful of fresh groceries that you can turn into a home-cooked gourmet meal. This method should prove effective, provided that you do the cooking and washing up.
